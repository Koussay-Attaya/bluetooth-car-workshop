# Bluetooth Car Workshop

> Community STEM Outreach | CPU Club Volunteer Workshop

---

# Overview

The Bluetooth Car Workshop was a volunteer STEM initiative organized under the CPU Club to introduce students to robotics, embedded systems, and wireless control through a hands-on engineering project.

During the workshop, participants designed, assembled, programmed, and tested their own Bluetooth-controlled robotic cars using Arduino. The objective was to demonstrate how software, electronics, and mechanical components interact to create an intelligent robotic system.

The workshop emphasized learning by doing, encouraging students to actively participate in every stage of the engineering process.

---

# Workshop Objectives

- Introduce students to robotics fundamentals.
- Teach the basics of embedded systems using Arduino.
- Explain wireless communication through Bluetooth.
- Develop programming and problem-solving skills.
- Encourage teamwork and engineering creativity.
- Build confidence through hands-on experimentation.

# My Role

---

# Workshop Content

Participants completed the following engineering workflow:

1. Introduction to robotics.
2. Understanding the electronic components.
3. Assembling the Bluetooth car.
4. Wiring the electronic circuit.
5. Uploading the Arduino program.
6. Pairing the Bluetooth module.
7. Testing the robot.
8. Debugging and improving performance.
